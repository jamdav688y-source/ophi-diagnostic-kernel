# Intervention Pattern: Constraint Hardening

## Use When

- boundaries are being bypassed
- incentives are degrading non-negotiable controls
- exceptions are becoming normalized

## What It Changes

Makes key constraints operationally binding through:
- stronger policy enforcement
- mandatory documentation
- approval gates
- violation thresholds

## What It Fixes

- constraint erosion
- hidden tradeoffs
- normalization of unsafe exceptions

## Costs Introduced

- slower throughput
- increased compliance workload
- less local discretion

## New Risks Introduced

- brittle process if overapplied
- shadow workarounds if enforcement lacks legitimacy

## Reversibility Profile

Moderate to High depending on scope.

## Escalation Requirement

Required when hardening affects broad operating capacity or organizational authority structure.
