# Intervention Pattern: Authority Rebinding

## Use When

- decision ownership is ambiguous
- stop authority is missing
- escalation routes are unclear
- multiple actors can act but no actor clearly owns outcome

## What It Changes

Establishes:
- named decision owner
- named stop-authority holder
- named escalation recipient
- scope of authority
- conditions under which authority transfers

## What It Fixes

- delayed action
- responsibility diffusion
- conflict at decision time
- governance gaps during failure

## Costs Introduced

- less informal flexibility
- more explicit accountability pressure
- potential slowdown if badly scoped

## New Risks Introduced

- overcentralization if authority is too narrow
- bottlenecks if escalation is overused

## Reversibility Profile

High, if authority definitions are documented and reviewed.

## Escalation Requirement

Required when rebinding changes strategic control or affects compliance, safety, or organizational power structure.
