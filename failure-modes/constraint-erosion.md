# Failure Mode: Constraint Erosion

## Definition

Non-negotiable boundaries weaken over time due to optimization pressure, expedience, incentives, or ambiguity.

## Detection Signals

- exceptions become normal
- policies are bypassed in practice
- speed is favored without explicit approval
- tradeoffs remain implicit
- safety or governance is treated as advisory only

## Why It Matters

Constraint erosion converts isolated exceptions into normalized risk.

## Risk Profile

Default band: Strategic  
Elevates to Systemic when:
- erosion affects legal, ethical, or governance boundaries
- no enforcement or escalation path exists
- intervention depends on informal correction

## Propagation Path

Pressure creates exceptions, exceptions reduce boundary clarity, reduced clarity encourages more exceptions, and control integrity decays.

## Primary Intervention Pattern

- constraint hardening
- escalation insertion

## Containment Logic

Reassert binding constraints, document exceptions, and require explicit escalation for deviation.
