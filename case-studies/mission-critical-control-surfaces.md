# Case Study: Mission-Critical Control Surfaces

## System Under Review

High-scale mission and production environment where throughput and operational validation are treated as indicators of readiness.

## Governing Objective

Determine whether mission readiness is supported by governable execution under fault conditions.

## Observed Signals

- emphasis on capacity and validation
- limited visibility into intervention pathways
- unclear rollback and containment references

## Diagnostic Extraction

Capability and deployment evidence are not sufficient to establish readiness at scale.

## Failure Classification

Primary:
- rollback infeasibility
- authority diffusion

Secondary:
- audit invisibility

## Risk Band

Strategic to Systemic due to mission and institutional consequence.

## Propagation Path

If throughput outpaces control surfaces, local degradation can escalate into systemic mission failure before intervention can stabilize the system.

## Proposed Intervention

- rollback path design
- authority rebinding
- observability upgrade
- escalation insertion

## Notes

Readiness is not just execution success. It is execution bounded by control, auditability, and intervention realism.
