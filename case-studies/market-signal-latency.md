# Case Study: Market Signal Latency

## System Under Review

Public market interpretation of a large enterprise experiencing revenue decline, margin pressure, and repricing.

## Governing Objective

Determine whether the problem is merely reported performance decline or deeper signal and response degradation.

## Observed Signals

- revenue contraction
- margin compression
- market repricing
- implied gap between operating reality and corrective action

## Diagnostic Extraction

The problem likely includes delayed ingestion or delayed response to meaningful market signals.

## Failure Classification

Primary:
- signal latency

Secondary:
- rollback infeasibility if correction options narrow with time

## Risk Band

Operational to Strategic depending on speed of correction and reversibility of response options.

## Propagation Path

Delayed feedback response compounds internal misalignment, increases correction cost, and lowers external confidence.

## Proposed Intervention

- observability upgrade
- escalation insertion
- reversible corrective design

## Notes

Markets often price not just results but the system’s apparent ability to correct itself.
