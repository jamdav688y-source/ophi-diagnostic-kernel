# Case Study: Enterprise AI Scaling

## System Under Review

Enterprise AI operating model shifting from isolated use cases to reusable agent classes.

## Governing Objective

Scale AI capability without losing coordination, control, or governance integrity.

## Observed Signals

- movement from use-case design to reusable agent architecture
- increased autonomy across distributed system functions
- stronger reuse narrative than governance narrative

## Diagnostic Extraction

The shift is not merely about scale.

It is a migration from localized execution to distributed autonomous decision-making.

## Failure Classification

Primary:
- distributed drift
- authority diffusion

Secondary:
- constraint erosion

## Risk Band

Strategic, with systemic potential if agents operate across shared domains without in-band control logic.

## Propagation Path

Local agent optimization diverges across domains, causing cross-system conflict, incoherence, and intervention delay.

## Proposed Intervention

- authority rebinding
- constraint hardening
- observability upgrade

## Notes

The central constraint in enterprise AI scaling is not reuse alone. It is coordinated autonomy under shared control.
