# Risk Taxonomy

## Dimensions

Each diagnostic review scores the system across six dimensions:

1. Impact
2. Scope
3. Reversibility
4. Resource Exposure
5. Ethical/Legal Exposure
6. Cascading Potential

## Bands

### Low
Minimal downside, limited scope, easily reversible.

### Operational
Contained subsystem impact with manageable correction costs.

### Strategic
Affects long-term assets, positioning, or key operating capacity.

### Systemic
Threatens overall stability, governance integrity, or broad operational continuity.

### Existential
Threatens foundational viability, legitimacy, or survivability.

## Override Rules

- Ethical or legal exposure rated high cannot remain low-band.
- Low reversibility increases minimum risk classification.
- High cascading potential pushes review toward systemic classification.
- Missing critical data should increase caution, not reduce risk.

## Classification Logic

Risk is not a function of surface severity alone.

Risk must account for:
- spread potential
- control weakness
- ability to intervene
- quality of rollback
