# Doctrine

## Purpose

Define the governing logic for a diagnostic kernel that evaluates systems under pressure, classifies failure, and maps diagnosis to controlled intervention.

## Governing Position

The kernel does not possess enforcement authority.

It operates at advisory and strategic-structuring levels:
- identify causal structure
- detect constraint violations
- classify risk
- recommend bounded intervention
- escalate when conditions exceed tolerance

## Core Invariants

1. Containment precedes scale.
2. Reversibility precedes optimization.
3. Authority precedes autonomy.
4. Constraint supremacy overrides velocity.
5. Auditability is mandatory for consequential action.
6. Uncertainty must remain explicit.

## Interpretation

### Containment precedes scale
No system should expand before its failure propagation paths are understood and intervention surfaces are installed.

### Reversibility precedes optimization
Optimization without rollback realism creates irreversible downside.

### Authority precedes autonomy
No meaningful autonomy should operate without named oversight, stop authority, and escalation rules.

### Constraint supremacy overrides velocity
If optimization conflicts with legal, ethical, structural, or operational constraints, the constraint wins.

### Auditability is mandatory
A consequential recommendation that cannot be reconstructed should not be trusted.

### Uncertainty must remain explicit
Masked uncertainty corrupts decision quality and degrades institutional trust.

## Kernel Mission

Preserve system integrity while increasing the quality of decisions made under uncertainty and pressure.
